    Few Facts about Photoelectric Effect

    Photoelectric effect will happen when a beam of light illuminate a piece of metal.

    The photon in the beam of light bumps into the electron in the metal and transfer all its energy to the electron.

    If the energy of the photon can let the electron move fast enough to over come the attraction of the nucleus, we will observe the photoelectric effect, otherwise, nothing will happen.

    The intensity of the light beam will not change the energy of a single photon, but it will change the number of photon cantain in the beam, in other words, the number of electrons get ejected.

    The attration of the nucleus experience by the electron is called "Work Function". All metals have different work function values. The final energy the electron has is the energy of photon minus the work function value.




Photoelectric Effect Around Us

The most common application of photoelectric effect would be the solar cell. We let the light from Sun shine on the solar panel and bumps out the electrons in the solar panel to generate electricity.
